More...

This course introduces students to graphic design as both an art medium and a form of communication. Students will produce communicative work in multiple formats (posters! zines! animations!) and explore visual problem-solving methods through a variety of in-class exercises. Participation in workshops, group discussions, guest lectures, and critiques will provide students with opportunities to develop their work with each other.

Tuesday, July 18

Typography: The Shape of Words

Exercise: The Shape of Words

Using the provided text block, scissors, and a gluestick, create typographic compositions that illustrate, amplify, re-arrange, or otherwise shift the meaning of the words in the text. Try to make 3 different versions that are as different as possible from each other. Your compositions should be made on an 8x8" square piece of paper, trimmed to size.

Exercise: Dots & Lines (finishes)

We'll pick up where we left off with the Dots & Lines exercise from week one, and do another quick pin-up!

Exercise: Adopt-a-Book

Neeta will bring in part of her zine collection—let's have a zine party in class! We'll look at small publications and discuss what we're responding to.

Project: Zine (starts)

Choose a Wikipedia page on something you’re fascinated by. Design a 16-page split-fold zine using any combination of the text and images on your chosen page. Consider other forms of text as well, such as footnotes, hyperlinks, references, external links, etc. What opportunities do they present? How can they be integrated, emphasized, highlighted, or made visible or invisible?

The Wikipedia page should be your foundation, but feel free to incorporate other forms of media and imagery to support your publication.
