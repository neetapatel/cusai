More...

This course introduces students to graphic design as both an art medium and a form of communication. Students will produce communicative work in multiple formats (posters! zines! animations!) and explore visual problem-solving methods through a variety of in-class exercises. Participation in workshops, group discussions, guest lectures, and critiques will provide students with opportunities to develop their work with each other.

Monday, July 24

Zine Construction

Project: Poster Series (finishes)

We will finish our group critique of the poster project this morning.

Demo: One Page Zines

We'll do a quick demo in class on how to construct a one-page zine from a tabloid-sized sheet of paper.

Workshop: Typesetting in InDesign

Then, we'll go up to the computer lab to learn how to use Adobe InDesign to create our zines. We'll set up the layout together, fill in some quick dummy content, and hit print!

Project: Zine (continues)

The rest of class will be reserved for working on our zines in the computer lab.


Tuesday, July 25

Zine Work Day

Project: Zine (continues)

Class time today will be dedicated to working on our zines! Our goal is to print and produce five copies by the end of the day.


Wednesday, July 26

Installation Day

Group Exhibition

With the exhibition in mind, today's class will be focused on finishing up and printing any remaining work on your posters and zines. Students who need to put more time into these projects will have access to the computer and print labs. In the meantime, Neeta and students who are finished their final touches will have the opportunity to pin up and organize work for the show!
